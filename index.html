Taking COMP.4610 - GUI Programming I

Why am I taking COMP.4610 - GUI Programming I this summer 2022?

I am taking COMP.4610 - GUI Programming I this summer 2022 for two reasons:

  1. I've learned that my Asperger's Syndrome makes me a very visual/spacial person. I already knew I was a visual/spacial person, but knowing this pushed me to further learn to interact through visual/spacial media. I see GUI as one of the top ways to communicate information, especially since I have ADHD which gives me a lower attention span. This can be seen in how I'm trying to keep the text narrow to make it easier to read.
  2. The COVID-19 pandemic forced me to stay at home which is not the best environment for me to be productive. I took virtual courses and failed most of them since I struggled to do anything course related outside of class. This messed with my remaining courses needed to graduate, pushed back my graduation, and ended up wasting a lot of money. By taking GUI I and GUI II over the summer, I can graduate a semester sooner instead of needing just one more semester just to take a single course.

What I hope to learn

I hope to learn about proper GUI techniques and vocabulary. Not only would this give me a better idea of what looks good visual/spacially, but also the words needed to properly express myself and understand others.

What I hope to accomplish

I hope to be able to create something I can both look at with pride and use in a portfolio. This webpage alone already feels great to make.

Why am I taking computer science as my major?

When I was younger I loved science (at least to the best an elementary schooler can understand what "science" even means). I started to beleive my favorite of the sciences was chemistry during my middle school years. I went to a vocation high school, Minuteman Regional Vocational Technical High School, where I pursued the biotech vocation since it was the closest to chemistry. All the chemicals, fluids, and cleanliness made me uncomfortable. My first year in college was liberal studies; I had no idea what to do for the future. My dad, a software engineer, convinced me to take an intro to computer science course for a semester. I enjoyed learning and applying what I learned, so much so that I decided to make it my major. There are still times where I question my path in life, but this is how I started my computer science major.

What do I enjoy about computer science?

When I can get my act together and work, I keep working and I work hard. I enjoy the learning process and I especially enjoy using what I just learned. Imagining the possible answers to the questions, "what can I use this for?" or "what are the possibilities?", is what makes me the most excited.
